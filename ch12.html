Chapter 13: Simulation and Modeling

Term definitions:

  1. Scientific Method: Observe, form hypothesis, test hypothesis, repeat
  2. Computational Model: A model simulated via a set of algorithmic procedures
  3. Discrete Event Simulation: Modeling the behavior of a system only at a finite set of times

List seven reasons to construct a simulation model

  1. Existence: Allows for experimenting on systems that do not yet exist.
  2. Physical Realization: Applicable for systems that cannot be represented by a physical model.
  3. Safety: It may be too dangerous to experiment on the actual system or a physical replica of it.
  4. Speed of construction: It is sometimes faster than building a physical model.
  5. Time scale: unlike with a physical model, the rate at which the system operates can be controlled.
  6. Ethics: Some physcial models present moral or ethical dilemmas which are not present with a simulated model.
  7. Ease of modification: Altering simulated models is much easier than reconstructing physical ones.
